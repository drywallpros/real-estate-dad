The playbook

It Doesn't Just Find the Deal.
It Walks You Through It.

Take a pre-foreclosure. The minute it hits the county record, the app hands you a step-by-step playbook, and it keeps you legal and fair the whole way.

1

It Finds the Filing

The app watches your county's public court records and flags a new pre-foreclosure the day it posts. You see it before the crowd does.

2

It Checks the Property

Owner, mortgage balance, other liens, and rough equity, so you know if there is a real deal before you spend a minute on it.

3

It Briefs You on the Situation

What stage the case is in, the timeline, and what the owner is facing, so you show up informed and respectful.

4

It Shows You How to Reach Out the Right Way

Who to contact, what to say, and what to avoid. Fair and lawful, never pressure. It respects Do-Not-Call and your state's rules.

5

It Helps You Make a Fair Offer

A fair-value range, not "just what they owe." It flags the equity the owner would give up, so your offer is one you can stand behind.

6

It Keeps You Legal

It flags the state rules that matter here: required disclosures, rescission rights, and whether you can assign the contract. It tells you when to bring in a title company or attorney.

7

It Keeps You on Track

Every next step lands on your calendar with a reminder, so the deal does not die in your inbox.

You Drive. The App Coaches.

The playbook guides you and reminds you. It does not chase homeowners or make offers for you, and it never pushes a pressure play. The whole point is to make you the calm, fair, prepared buyer, not the fifteenth spam call.

Funding and leverage

Finding the Deal Is Half.
Funding It Is the Other Half.

A great deal you cannot pay for is not a deal. So for every property it finds, the app lays out the ways to fund it, from bank loans to seller financing to money you already control, and points you toward the ones worth a closer look for this deal.

Your edge

Leverage You Already Have

Most investors have to go find money. You are sitting on it. The app starts here because this is the cheapest, fastest leverage you own.

  • • Equity in real estate you already own, pulled out with a cash-out refinance or a HELOC
  • • Your own crews rehab at cost: drywall, paint, roofing, HVAC, and mold. That is real profit others pay for
  • • Trade credit and supplier accounts you already run for materials
  • • Your track record and relationships, which get you better terms

Bank and Lender Loans

  • • Conventional investment property loans
  • • DSCR loans that qualify on the rent, not your paycheck
  • • Hard money, fast and asset based, built for flips
  • • Bridge loans to move before you sell
  • • Portfolio and blanket loans across many properties
  • • Commercial loans for five units and up

Renovation and Rehab Money

  • • FHA 203k renovation loans
  • • Fannie Mae HomeStyle loans
  • • Construction to permanent loans
  • • Business line of credit for materials and labor
  • • Zero percent cards for short-term rehab spend
  • • Gap and transactional funding to cover the middle

Creative Financing (Little to No Bank)

  • • Seller financing, where the seller becomes the bank
  • • Subject to, taking over the existing payments
  • • Lease option and rent to own
  • • Land contract and contract for deed
  • • Wraparound mortgage
  • • BRRRR, so one pile of cash buys deal after deal
  • • Wholesaling and assignment, control it with a contract

Other People's Money and Partners

  • • Private money lenders
  • • Joint venture and equity partners: they fund, you bring the deal and the crews
  • • Credit partners and co-signers
  • • Pooling several investors, with the legal paperwork done right

Money You Already Control

  • • Self-directed IRA or Solo 401k to buy inside your retirement
  • • A loan against your 401k
  • • A loan against a whole life policy
  • • A line of credit against investments you hold

Programs, Grants, and Tax Leverage

  • • Down payment assistance and renovation grants
  • • Opportunity Zones and first look programs
  • • Land banks and local redevelopment incentives
  • • Historic and energy rebates
  • • SBA loans for the business side
  • • 1031 exchange to roll gains into the next property and defer the tax
  • • Cost segregation and depreciation to cut the tax bill
